CODEVID-19

With the COVID-19 pandemic in full swing, many people are looking at a lot of free time stuck indoors. Avoid cabin fever and use that time to make a difference in your community and the fight against COVID-19 by joining CODEVID-19, the world’s first pandemic hackathon!

The purpose of the hackathon ultimately isn’t to win prizes, it’s to build useful apps that people can use to manage and survive during the COVID-19 pandemic Like the COVID-19 situation the hackathon resources and structure will change as time goes on!

The first prize (called the first responder prize) will be awarded March 24th and there will be many more to come!

Rules + Submission info + FAQ

Team size
- up to 5 people
Intellectual Property (IP)
- IP entered is owned by teams
Teams must ensure they have rights to use the libraries, assets and source code
Must be able to produce proof or rights if requested, but not required to submit
When can you sign up?
- Any day before late April (final end date and competition TBD)
Does the app need to be open source?
- No, apps don’t need to be open source
- Judges may ask to scrutinize source code to verify technical claims, and failure to do so could impact judging
Submission process
- The app doesn’t need to be complete
- To be eligible a submission MUST include:
  1. a link to a working app
  2. screenshot
  3. directions to use the app (if not included in the app itself)
  4. a point of contact for the judges to ask questions
- Submissions MAY include:
  1. OPTIONAL: A link to a source code repository (GitHub, Gitlab, etc.)
In the spirit of a hackathon, only apps developed during the hackathon are eligible for prizes though we're happy to spotlight COVID-19 related apps and services developed outside it.
Weekly Prizes
- Submissions for the weekly prizes must be in Sunday by 23:59 UTC.
- Projects submit their application to the weekly competition using the submission form
- Voting is by popular vote and open to the public, starting Monday 03:00 UTC, ending Tuesday 03:00 UTC (24 hours to vote)
- Tell your friends and family to vote. Spread the word about CODEVID-19!
- Each weeks winner is selected from the entries
- Winners announced by Tuesday, 20:00 UTC
- Weekly prizes are smaller and mostly for bragging rights
- Organizers reserve the right to disqualify entries and winners who violate the code of conduct and submission rules as outlined
- Sponsors are also able to offer prizes at their discretion during the event (to be announced at a later date)
- The first week is called the first responder prize! Go get those bragging rights!
- You can submit to multiple weeks but do not submit the exact same project! Make sure you have made some progress!
Final Competition
- Contest ends at the end of April
- Final prize categories and judging criteria TBD in the coming weeks
- Final judging will start May 1st, and finish when all entries are judged, depending on submissions
Resources
- This will be a staggered launch and there will be things changing as you go (more datasets, partners, etc) just like the viruses change
Can you participate without being judged?
- Absolutely! Just don’t submit a link to be judged
Code of Conduct
- Apps need to respect Code of Conduct as well
- All team members and participants agree to this
- If you have questions please ask
Cost
- There is no entry or participant fee
Timing
- All times are in UTC
Judging
- Judges may be added as we find experts and sponsors
- The final Competition in April will most likely have judges and judging criteria so stay tuned!
- Participants understand that this is a charitable event organized by volunteers with limited resources and time. As such, participants understand that a best effort will be made to ensure fairness and equality in the judging and awarding of prizes. Participants accept that prizes will be awarded at the discretion of the judges, sponsors, and organizers. By participating all participants agree to accept the decisions of the judges, sponsors and organizers, and waive all rights to pursue legal or other recourse against the event, organizers, judges, sponsors, partners, or other parties should a dispute arise.
